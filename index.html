AURUM User Guide
Mastering Analysis Suite — Quick Reference

Getting Started

AURUM is a browser-based mastering analysis suite. It runs entirely in your browser — no server, no uploads. All audio stays on your device.

Step 1 — Load Audio

Drag and drop any audio file onto the top bar, or click the drop zone to browse. Supported formats: WAV · MP3 · FLAC · AIFF and most formats your browser supports.

Step 2 — Press Play

Hit the gold play button or press Space. The spectrum, phase correlator, and LUFS meters all update live as the audio plays. Integrated LUFS and LRA are computed from the full file in the background immediately after loading.

Step 3 — Analyse & Compare

Select a streaming platform target in the right column. AURUM shows how far your track is from that target and gives a plain-language mastering recommendation. Use A/B slots to compare two different loudness treatments side-by-side.

Pro tip: Offline analysis (Integrated LUFS, LRA, True Peak) runs the moment you load a file — no need to play through the whole track. Just press play to watch the real-time meters.

Player & Loading

AURUM's mini player gives you precision transport control and waveform scrubbing.

Waveform

The waveform display shows the raw amplitude of channel 1. Click anywhere on the waveform or the thin progress bar below it to jump to that position.

Output Volume

The Out slider adjusts playback gain up to +3.5 dB. This does not affect the export — use it to compensate for monitor gain differences. The signal is protected by a safety limiter at −0.5 dBFS.

File Info

Below the transport, AURUM displays sample rate, channel count, duration, and file size — useful to confirm what format you actually loaded.

Scrubbing: Click the progress bar to seek. Playback restarts instantly from that point — no silence, no pop.

Stereo & Space

The Stereo & Space panel gives you M/S width control, Haas widening, reverb, and delay. Changes take effect immediately in the next playback — press play after adjusting.

ControlDescription
Stereo WidthM/S matrix width control. 100% = original. Below 100% narrows toward mono; above 100% widens the side signal.
Haas DelayAdds a short delay (0–40 ms) to the right channel, creating psychoacoustic width without altering the mono sum.
Room TypeSelects the reverb IR character: Small Room, Medium Hall, Large Hall, Plate, Chamber, Cathedral.
Dry/WetReverb mix — how much of the wet signal blends with the dry. 0% = fully dry.
DecayReverb tail length in seconds (0.1 – 10 s).
Delay TimeEcho repeat time in milliseconds (10 – 1000 ms).
FeedbackHow much of each echo feeds back into the next repeat (0–90%).
Delay MixWet level of the echo effect.
Mastering note: For transparency, leave Stereo Width at 100% and Haas at 0 unless you have a specific spatial goal. Heavy widening can cause mono compatibility issues — check the Phase Correlator while adjusting.

Spectrum Analysis

Three analysis modes reveal frequency content in different ways. Switch between them with the segmented control in the panel header.

Spectrum (Bars)

Classic FFT bar display. Bars are colour-coded by intensity. Good for a quick read of energy distribution — check that your sub isn't too dominant and the high-end isn't harsh.

Line (Spectrum)

Smooth FFT line trace. Easier to read gentle slopes and compare frequency balance across a full mix. Toggle Log/Lin for logarithmic vs linear frequency axis.

Spectrogram

Time-scrolling waterfall with frequency on the vertical axis and time moving left-to-right. Colour intensity shows amplitude. Excellent for detecting noise, clicks, breathiness, or tonal buildup over time.

True Peak

The highest inter-sample peak in dBTP. For streaming, target −1 dBTP or lower. Shown in red if over −1.

Loudness Range (LRA)

Dynamic range of the programme (LU). Typical music: 6–12 LU. Higher = more dynamic; lower = more compressed.

Crest Factor

Peak-to-RMS ratio in dB. A high crest factor (> 15 dB) means lots of dynamic headroom; low values (< 6 dB) indicate heavy limiting or clipping. Shown in red if the mix appears clipped.

Phase Correlator

The Phase Correlator shows the stereo relationship between your left and right channels in real time.

Goniometer (Lissajous)

The oval/circular trace represents stereo content. A perfectly vertical line = pure mono. A perfect circle = fully decorrelated stereo. A horizontal line = out-of-phase (sum cancels to zero). Healthy stereo mix appears as a slightly vertical ellipse.

Correlation Meter

Needle ranges from −1 (out of phase) through 0 (wide/decorrelated) to +1 (mono). For streaming masters, stay above 0 to ensure mono compatibility. Values below −0.2 will cause bass cancellation on mono playback.

Stereo Width Meter

A simple bar reading derived from the side-to-mid energy ratio. 50% = balanced mono-compatible stereo. 100% = very wide.

Status colours: GOOD correlation above +0.3 — mono-safe. WIDE 0 to +0.3 — borderline. PHASE ISSUE below 0 — check for polarity or M/S processing errors.

Loudness & LUFS

AURUM implements full ITU-R BS.1770-4 K-weighted loudness metering. Offline analysis gives you accurate integrated readings from the full file the moment it loads.

MeterWindowUse
Momentary400 msShows instant loudness. Useful for catching peaks in dynamic passages.
Short-Term3 sShows loudness over the last 3 seconds. Good for verse/chorus comparison.
IntegratedFull fileThe number that streaming platforms use for normalisation. This is your target.
Platform Targets

Click any platform row to select it as your reference. The coloured delta value shows how far your integrated LUFS is from that target.

ON TARGET within ±0.5 LU — leave it. WARN within ±2 LU — minor adjustment advised. OFF more than 2 LU — significant loudness mismatch.

Presets & A/B

Save and recall different processing states to quickly compare settings or pick up where you left off.

A/B Comparison Slots

Two memory slots — A and B — store a complete snapshot of all controls. Use them to compare two approaches (e.g. wide vs narrow stereo, or different reverb settings) without losing either state.

Save to Slot snapshots the current settings into the active slot (the highlighted one — A or B). A green dot appears when a slot has data. Copy A→B duplicates slot A into B for incremental comparison.

Factory Presets

Pre-built starting points for common mastering scenarios: Club Loud, Broadcast Safe, Warm & Wide, Transparent, Intimate Acoustic, and more. Click to instantly apply. They're overridable — adjust any control after applying.

User Presets

Save your own presets with custom names. They persist across sessions (stored in your browser's localStorage). Use 💾 Save to name and store the current state; 🗑 Clear removes all user presets after confirmation.

Shortcut: Press A or B on your keyboard to instantly recall slot A or B while playing — great for real-time A/B comparison during critical listening.

Export & Workflow

Once you've loaded a file and adjusted settings, you can export a processed WAV. The export button appears automatically after a file is loaded.

⬇ Export WAV

Renders the loaded audio through the Master Volume gain only, then encodes a 16-bit PCM WAV file at the original sample rate. The file is named aurum_export_tuned.wav and downloads to your default folder.

Note: The current export applies the Output Volume control. The Stereo Width, Reverb, Delay, and Haas controls affect playback only and are not baked into the exported file — use AURUM as an analysis and reference tool, then apply confirmed settings in your DAW.

Recommended Workflow

1. Load your near-final mix or master candidate into AURUM.

2. Check Integrated LUFS against your target platform — adjust limiting/gain in your DAW.

3. Verify True Peak is below −1 dBTP. Check Phase Correlation is above 0.

4. Review the Spectrum and Spectrogram for tonal balance issues and artifacts.

5. Use A/B slots to compare your master with a reference track.

6. When happy, export your final version from your DAW at 24-bit/44.1 kHz or higher.
